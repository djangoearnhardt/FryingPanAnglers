Ed Deison Senior Guide

Ed started fishing in the North East with his father as soon as he could walk. Saltwater, freshwater, it didn't matter.
After graduating from the University of West Virginia in Forestry and Wildlife management, Ed headed out west and settled first in Boulder in 1993 fishing all the rivers on the Front Range.

It didn't take long though before he discovered the Roaring Fork Valley moving here and commencing guiding in 1997.

He has fished here ever since. Over the winters he heads to Florida for saltwater fishing as well.

Ed has a Facebook page so you can talk to him there as well as on the Frying Pan Anglers Facebook page.

Harry Wildman

Harry grew up in Sea Isle city, New Jersey and started saltwater surf and pier fishing with his grandfather and father at age four. Harry worked summers at bait and tackle shops in Sea Isle (Minmar, John's Pier).
He then spent the next three summers working on the Miss Townsend's inlet charter fishing boat.

Harry moved west in 1990 and wound up in the Roaring Fork Valley in 1993.

He started fly fishing for trout in 1990 and became addicted. Harry started working for Frying Pan Anglers in 2002. "Fly fishing for trout is like playing chess with Mother Nature".

Cameron Rico Suave

Cameron Started fishing at the age of 10 in Northern Michigan and started guiding for steelhead, smallmouth and trout 15 years ago. After graduating from Oakland Community College he worked at Bass Pro in their fly fishing department teaching fly fishing and fly tying.

Coming to Colorado in 2004 he worked at Elk Trout Lodge an Orvis endorsed guide service and lodge and then joined Frying Pan Anglers where he has found his home.

He loves all waters from the small streams to the big waters of the Colorado. Cameron also travels the country in the off season giving presentations to fly clubs and fly fishermen on the Roaring Fork and Frying Pan watersheds. Patient, a great sense of humor Cameron has been married for 9 years to Kelly and has a great little girl, Addy, who already accompanies him on fishing trips on his days off. Cameron has a Facebook page under Cameron Cipponeri. You can talk to him there as well as on the Frying Pan Anglers Facebook page. Cameron also has his own website.

Jordan Geuax-rdon

Jordan Duchein was born and raised in Baton Rouge, Louisiana where he spent all his free time catching bass, bream and sac au lait (Cajun French for crappie) as well as saltwater catches of speckled trout and redfish in the marshes of southern Louisiana. The summers of his youth were spent in Aspen where he fell in love with fishing in the cold rivers and high mountain lakes of Colorado.

After enlisting in the USMC infantry and serving in Iraq, he graduated from Colorado State University.

Soon after he followed his passion to his favorite place in the world,the Roaring Fork Valley. His favorite thing in the world is to help people to catch fish and to share the magic, tranquility, and beauty that is the Roaring Fork Valley.

Marty Shop Manager

Growing up in Ohio Marty enjoyed many types of fishing, from fishing Bass on lakes and streams to walleye fishing on the Great Lakes to crappie fishing my home town lake and of course trout fishing. Fishing most of his life, Marty owes his passion to his grand father who used to take him and his brother fishing every weekend to a lake, stream or reservoir.
Marty has a Mathematics degree from The Ohio State University, (Go Buckeyes), and after college decided to move
to the Rocky Mountains.

His Instagram handle is @Martyj2405.

He loves the outdoors and chasing beautiful trout in an exceptionally gorgeous landscape. He enjoys dry fly fishing as well as tugging big streamers on our gold medal Rivers here in the Roaring Fork Valley.
So if you are looking for a positive laid back educational fishing experience, he would love to show you around our local Rivers.
